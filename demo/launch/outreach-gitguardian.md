I'm building ContextDuty (https://github.com/SHUBHAGYTA24/contextduty), an open-source tool that prevents secrets from leaking into AI coding assistants. I think there's a clean co-marketing story here and wanted to reach out.

**Where we're different:**

GitGuardian is the gold standard for catching secrets after they enter git history — you scan commits, PRs, and repositories. That's a critical layer.

ContextDuty works upstream of git: it prevents secrets from reaching AI tools (Cursor, Copilot, Windsurf, etc.) and blocks them at the pre-commit stage so they never enter history in the first place. We also have an HTTPS proxy that intercepts secrets from AI API calls in real-time.

Together, the story is: "GitGuardian watches your history. ContextDuty guards the moment before."

"Two layers, complete coverage: how GitGuardian and ContextDuty stop secrets at every stage of the AI-assisted dev workflow"

I'd cover: the new threat surface (AI tools indexing workspaces), how ContextDuty handles pre-commit and context interception, how GitGuardian covers repo history and CI/CD scanning — and how they complement each other rather than overlap.
